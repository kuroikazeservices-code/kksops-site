Built from nothing, for the ones building from nothing.

On the name: KuroiKaze, the Black Wind, comes from an old story. In that story, the Black Wind was a harbinger of change. We kept that. The tools we build and the support we offer are meant to fill the sails of people who want to chase their dreams or find their own place in the world.

Where this started

KuroiKaze Services didn't start with a business plan. It started with a breaking point.

After years of pouring everything into jobs that didn't reciprocate, including a final employer who crossed lines that couldn't be uncrossed, working for someone else stopped being an option. For an AuDHD brain, the unpredictability of toxic employment isn't just unpleasant. It's unsustainable.

The pivot was to contract work. Clear goals. Defined problems. Real output. But that raised an immediate question: how do you invoice on the go, quickly, professionally, without dragging out a laptop every time? The apps on the market were either too heavy, too expensive, or built for someone else entirely. So we built one.

"How many other people out there want to work for themselves and just need a little help so they can get paid for the work they do?"

That question became the company.

Who we build for

Our tools are built for solo operators and microbusinesses: the people who don't have IT departments, onboarding budgets, or time to climb a learning curve. Pet sitters. Tradespeople. Caregivers. Freelancers of every stripe.

Our first invoicing app was inspired by interactions with freelance nursing, caregiving, and family support professionals, some of them working seven days a week just to survive. Professional-looking invoices aren't just paperwork. They're leverage. They give people the confidence to charge what their work is actually worth.

If an app can give someone back even a little time and a little confidence, that matters.

Where we're going

KuroiKaze Services is building a focused suite of industry-specific invoicing apps, each one built on a shared foundation and tooled toward the specific needs and workflow of its users. After that, expanded functionality. After that, whatever else the people we serve actually need.

The longer arc is bigger than software. The goal has always been to build something that generates enough to give back, funding advocacy and support for neurodivergent people who have been failed by systems that weren't designed with them in mind. That work is personal. It always will be.

You don't have to do it alone.
